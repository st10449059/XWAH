First Aid

First aid is the initial assistance provided to someone experiencing a medical emergency. It aims to preserve life, prevent the condition from worsening, and promote recovery until professional medical help arrives. A first aid class equips participants with essential skills and knowledge to effectively respond to emergencies, fostering confidence and readiness to act in critical situations. It's a valuable course for anyone, regardless of their background or profession.

Concepts covered in the first aid class:

  1. Introduction to First Aid:
    • Understanding the role of first aid and its importance
    • Legal considerations and consent

    1. Assessment of the Scene:
      • Ensuring safety for yourself and the victim
      • Performing a quick assessment of the situation

      1. Basic Life Support (BLS):
        • CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) techniques for adults, children, and infants
        • Use of an Automated External Defibrillator (AED)

        1. Choking Relief:
          • Techniques for assisting adults, children, and infants who are choking

          1. Wound Care:
            • Types of wounds (abrasions, lacerations, punctures)
            • Cleaning, dressing, and infection prevention

            1. Burn Treatment:
              • Identifying types and severity of burns
              • Appropriate first aid for minor and major burns

              1. Managing Shock:
                • Recognizing signs of shock
                • Steps to take to help the victim

                1. Responding to Common Emergencies:
                  • Recognizing and responding to heart attacks, strokes, and seizures
                  • Treatment for allergic reactions and anaphylaxis

                  1. Injury Management:
                    • Sprains, strains, fractures, and dislocations
                    • R.I.C.E. method (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation)

                    1. Environmental Emergencies:
                      • Heat-related illnesses (heat exhaustion, heat stroke)
                      • Cold-related emergencies (hypothermia, frostbite)

                      1. Special Considerations:
                        • First aid for children, elderly, and pregnant individuals
                        • Basic mental health first aid

                        Practical Experience:

                        • Hands-on practice of CPR and other techniques using mannequins and first aid kits.
                        • Scenario-based training to simulate real-life emergencies.
                        • Assessments and drills to reinforce learning.
